### B1. 基本（スタッフ囲み、スニーカー履き替え）

```
Transform this fashion photo into a candid behind-the-scenes off-shot.
Use the attached face reference to match the model's face exactly — same facial features, bone structure, skin tone, hairstyle, MAKEUP, and MOLE/BEAUTY MARK POSITIONS.
The model has changed into the SNEAKERS shown in the attached sneaker reference photo. The sneakers must match the reference exactly.
IMPORTANT: Only the sneakers should be visible as footwear. Do NOT include any other shoes anywhere in the frame.

CAMERA: Shot on Leica M6, Summicron 35mm f/2, Kodak Portra 400 film. Handheld — slightly imperfect focus, natural motion blur allowed.

WHAT TO CHANGE:
- The model is NOT posing. She is caught in a private moment — drinking coffee, checking her phone, laughing at something off-camera, adjusting her clothes, or zoning out between takes.
- Her posture is RELAXED and UNPOSED — slouching, leaning, weight on one leg, arms crossed, one hand in her hair.
- Add 2-3 CREW MEMBERS partially visible at the edges of frame: a hair stylist with a brush, a photographer checking his camera LCD, a boom mic operator, an assistant holding a reflector. They should feel natural, not staged.
- Add REAL SET CLUTTER on the ground and in the background: cables, a power strip, paper coffee cups, a script/call sheet, equipment cases, sandbags, a folded reflector, coats draped over chairs.
- Lighting is AMBIENT ONLY — overhead practicals, window light, or a single bare bulb. No studio softboxes aimed at the model. Lighting equipment may be visible but should be powered off or pointing away.
- Framing is IMPERFECT — the model may be slightly off-center, with extra headroom or a slight horizon tilt. Shot quickly without careful composition.

FILM QUALITY:
- Strong visible grain (Portra 400 pushed)
- Slightly warm color cast
- Soft focus edges, sharp center
- Available light only — no flash

The overall feeling should be intimate and voyeuristic, like a diary photo from a photographer's contact sheet that was never meant to be published. NOT a magazine spread.
```
